Max Temperature

This plot shows the relationship between the max temperature recorded in a city and the lattitude of the city. as we can see from the data their is a strong exponential correlation, as cities closer to the equator typically record higher max temperatures throughout the year. This phenominon can be explained by the fact that the suns rays are cast more directly closer to the middle of the earth. Conversly, the farther north (on the graph higher in Latitude) we look, temperatures become colder, as the suns rays hit these areas at an angle meaning they are less focused and have less impact on the temperature in the area.
